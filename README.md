# 📊 Employee Attrition Analytics & Prediction

## 📌 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing employee data to identify key factors influencing attrition and building a machine learning model to predict employee turnover. The goal is to help organizations make data-driven HR decisions to improve employee retention and workforce planning.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze employee performance and attrition trends
* Identify key factors affecting employee retention
* Build predictive models to identify employees at risk of leaving
* Provide actionable insights for HR decision-making

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies

* **Python** (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn)
* **Data Visualization** (Matplotlib, Seaborn)
* **SQL** (for data analysis – to be added)
* **Power BI** (for dashboard visualization – to be added)

---

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset used is the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset, which contains information about employee demographics, job roles, salary, performance, and attrition status.

---

## 🔍 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Performed data cleaning and exploratory analysis to uncover patterns and relationships between employee attributes and attrition.

### Key analysis areas:

* Attrition distribution
* Department-wise performance
* Overtime impact on attrition
* Salary vs attrition relationship

---

## 📊 Key Insights

* Employees working overtime are more likely to leave the organization
* Lower salary is strongly associated with higher attrition
* Job satisfaction plays a critical role in employee retention
* Certain departments show higher attrition trends

---

## 🤖 Machine Learning Models

* Logistic Regression
* Random Forest Classifier

The models were trained to predict employee attrition based on various features.

---

## 📈 Model Performance

The models demonstrated strong capability in identifying employees at risk of attrition, enabling proactive HR interventions.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

* Improve employee satisfaction and engagement programs
* Reduce excessive overtime workload
* Review compensation structure for lower-income employees
* Focus on early-stage employee retention strategies
